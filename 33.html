The Strange Case of Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde The Mysterious Death of Dr Lanyon Time passed. Thousands of pounds were offered as a reward for any information, but Mr Hyde had disappeared as if he had never existed. Much of his past was dug up, and it was all shameful. Stories were told of his pitiless cruelty and violence, of his immoral life, of his strange behaviour, of the hatred that seemed to have surrounded him. But not a whisper was heard of his present hiding place. From the time he left the house in Soho on the morning of the murder, he had simply melted into the London fog. Gradually, as the weeks passed, Mr Utterson began to forget his fears. The death of Sir Danvers was, in his opinion, fully paid for by the disappearance of Hyde. At the same time a new life began for Dr Jekyll, now that Hyde's evil influence had been removed.
